    He has an MSc and BSc in Public Health from the University of Ghana and University of Health and Allied Sciences respectively. He has over 5 years of research and professional experience in the health sector. As a Graduate Researcher at the Faculty of Medical Sciences, UM6P, Fidelis showcased his prowess in research by conducting a study on how uniquely Collective Intelligence could be applied in implementing Universal Health Care in Morocco, analyzing health policies and reviewing Randomised Controlled Trials (RCTs) evidence on non-communicable diseases in developing countries. His keen analytical skills were further honed at J-PAL, Morocco Employment Lab, where he delved into policies targeting non-communicable diseases. Fidelis has an embroidered diversity of professional experiences, ranging from his role as a Senior Public Health Officer at the University of Ghana Medical Centre Ltd to his position as an Assistant Project Manager at Mohammed VI Polytechnic University. He also worked with institutions, including, Marie Stopes International Ghana, SEND Ghana, and Ghana Health Service, just to mention a few.
